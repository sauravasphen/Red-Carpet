Pikey Peak Trek
Main Destination :
Pikey Base Camp
Duration :
8 Days
Starting from :
USD 699
Trip Overview
  • Monasteries take you on a spiritual journey with peaceful energy and exciting history.
  • Diverse flora and fauna with dense forests create a different world for the visitor.
  • Moderate to easy trails make the trip easier for even new trekkers.
  • Rustic teahouses that teach you to find comfort in simplicity
  • Breathtaking mountain views will accompany you on your entire journey.
  • Lush pasture lands and locals with colorful culture and festivals that have their mythical importance
  • Cuisines that will have you licking your fingers at the end of every meal
Pikey Peak trek is an exciting and challenging journey through the stunning Himalayan region of Nepal. This itinerary takes you on a journey through some of the most beautiful and picturesque landscapes in the world, with spectacular views of the peaks of the Everest range and others.

Pike Peak trek begins in the bustling city of Kathmandu, where you will have a chance to explore the many temples and markets before setting out on your journey. From Kathmandu, you will ride to Dhap, which serves as the gateway to the Solu region.

From Dhap, you will embark on a Pikey Peak trek through the lush green forests and rugged terrain to Jhapre. Along the way, you will see breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys and an up-close look at the stunning flora and fauna that thrive in this region.

As you make your way through the region, you will have the opportunity to explore the beautiful Sigane village. From Sigane, you will continue to the Jhapre Village. Here, you will have the chance to visit the local markets and try delicious local cuisine.

From Jhapre, you will make your way to the Pikey Peak Base camp. You will continue to the Pikey Peak summit from the Base camp. You will be treated to breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys and the beautiful sunrise among the Himalayas.

After exploring the pikey base camp, you will continue to the beautiful Loding and then to Junbesi, where you can visit the local monastery and learn about the rich Buddhist culture of the region. From here, you will make your way to Taksindu, where you will have the opportunity to witness the three famous mountain ranges Everest, Chamlang, and Makalu. After that, you will head to Phaplu and finally reach Ringmo after crossing a small stream. Ringmo is famous for its beautiful apple orchards and organic apple products. After Ringmo, you will continue Pikey Peak trek downward to Taksindu La, known for the Taksindu Monastery, where you will have the opportunity to visit the local monastery and learn about the rich history and culture of this region as well as the famous festival, “Mani Rimdu.”

From Taksindu, you will head to Phaplu, your last trip destination, where you can explore another monastery called Chiwong Gompa.

Pike Peak trek Trails is an incredible journey that takes you through some of the world’s most beautiful and picturesque landscapes. Along the way, you will have the opportunity to interact with the local communities and learn about their way of life, as well as explore the stunning natural beauty of the Solu region. With its moderately challenging terrain and breathtaking views, this trek will surely be a memorable experience you will treasure for a lifetime.

Pike Peak is an alternative trek to Everest Base Camp for those who want to experience and see the sherpa culture and Mt. Everest, including other Himalayas. It is the best option for those who do not want to have more adventure but want to enjoy the local culture, cushion, and hilly areas with the picture backdrop views of Himalayan ranges from a small beautiful village.
Trip Itinerary
Day 1 : Arrival
As you arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, a representative will pick you up and transfer you to your hotel. Our trek leader will give you a brief orientation about the activities and itineraries of the trip. If you arrive early, you can walk around the city, hunting for street food and souvenirs, or stroll through Thamel. Your tour manager can help you make arrangements.

Overnight stay in Kathmandu.
Day 2 : Drive from Kathmandu to Dhap
Today you will drive to Dhap, an 8-9 hours trip full of beautiful countryside. From the south of Dhap, you will start your first trek of the journey. You will walk on the offbeat trail and reach a place called Sigane. You will see your first glimpse of the mountain range, the Numbur Himal. After a couple of hours of trekking, you will reach your final stop for the day, Jhapre. You can relax or go exploring the Gompa and Stupa that this place is famous for.
Day 3 : Jhapre to Pikey Peak Base Camp
You start your day with a trail snaking uphill to Bhulbhule. After reaching Bhulbhule, a nameless lodge offers spectacular views of the Everest range. Bhulbhule is your overnight stop for tonight. Relax and enjoy the remoteness of your surroundings.
Day 4 : Sunrise in the Pike Peak
It’s early morning, and you need to prepare yourself for the beautiful sunrise you are bound to cherish for your life even after this journey. Yes, you have a pending climb towards the viewpoint which offers you sunrise and, on clear days, panoramic views of other mountain ranges such as Annapurna and Kanchnajungha. After Pikey peak, you will follow a steep trail that eventually leads forest known for the Rhododendron blooms during springtime. Walk for a while and reach Jase Bhanjyang, where breakfast will be waiting for you. After breakfast, you will continue walking toward Loding, your overnight stop. Here you will have a few more diverse options for accommodations.
Day 5 : From Loding to Junbesi
From Loding, you will start your hike upwards to Junbesi through trails surrounded by prayer flags and holy inscriptions. A tiny settlement nestled in the base with quaint little houses and teahouses. You also have the option to visit the Thubtenchoching Monastery, a peaceful spot known for its spiritual aura and environment.
Day 6 : Junbesi to Taksindu
After breakfast, you cross a creek and then enter forest trails, eventually taking you to Sailung. Sailung offers a 360 view of mountains like Everest, Chamlang, and Makalu. You walk downwards to Phaplu, continue to the trail, and finally reach Ringmo after crossing a small stream. Ringmo is known for its beautiful apple orchards and products like apple cider, apple pies, and more. After Ringmo, you will continue trekking to Taksindu La, known for the Taksindu Monastery. Built in the 1940s, the monastery emanates peaceful and spiritual energy. You’d be staying overnight at Taksindu.
Day 7 : From Taksindu to Phaplu
You take a different route from the trekking route today and walk downwards to Phaplu, your overnight stop. You can also hike up to Chiwong Gompa, which is a must if traveling around autumn since the monastery celebrates Mani Rimdu like no other place.
Day 8 : Drive back to Kathmandu from Phaplu
You will drive back to Kathmandu on your final trip day. After reaching the capital, you will have some time on your hand. You can either take a walk on the streets of Kathmandu and soak in the harmony of the chaos or relax in your room.
Day 9 : Departure
Today is the last day of our trip. On our last day, our representative from the red-carpet journey will help you with your check-out process and assist you to the hotel. Before departing, ensure you have all your essential travel documents and other necessities.
